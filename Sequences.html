Be very careful of the idiom

1:length(x)

What if x has length 0? Then this is 1:0 and we get two elements. So y[1:length(x)] would yeild y[c(1, 0)] and give the first element.

What about

for(i in 1:length(x)) {
 ...
}

This would give two iterations where i is 1 and then 0.

Instead, we nearly always want

seq(along = x)
